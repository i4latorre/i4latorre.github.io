About

I am a theoretical physicist concerned with the architecture of quantum and electromagnetic systems, with particular attention to symmetry, irreversibility and the subtle ways structure governs dynamics.
My formation ranges from experimental work in materials synthesis to the formulation of relativistic open-system models and the classical emulation of quantum phenomena. This trajectory has unfolded across laboratory practice, analytical theory and computational simulation, and has included teaching and coaching roles at multiple academic levels.
I pursue research that links conceptual clarity with technological relevance, and I maintain, parallel to my scientific work, a continued engagement with musical study and production as a counterpoint that sharpens perception and discipline.
