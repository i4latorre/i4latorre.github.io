Research Landscape

My work sits at the intersection of quantum transport, complex networks, and non-equilibrium phenomena, with a recurring focus on symmetry, irreversibility, and data-informed modeling across physical and chemical systems. In parallel, I am increasingly interested in the development of scientific SaaS: turning rigorous models, simulations, and data pipelines into scalable, reproducible, and user-facing computational tools that bridge research and application. Alongside my scientific work, I maintain an ongoing engagement with musical study and production as a counterpoint that sharpens perception and discipline.

Research Profile and Technical Expertise

The research profile presented here spans quantum transport in molecular, nanoscale, and graphene-based systems, including geometry-driven conduction, coherence phenomena, and transport in fractal and graph-based structures. Experience includes analytical modeling, numerical simulation, and spectral analysis of quantum graphs relevant to molecular electronics and solid-state physics.

Additional expertise includes the statistical analysis of large-scale Human Connectome Project fMRI datasets, with emphasis on time series analysis, correlation structure, network science, and graph-based modeling of complex systems. Research focuses on identifying signatures of criticality, power-law behavior, and emergent dynamics in high-dimensional biological data.

Ongoing work integrates physics-informed machine learning and AI-driven modeling for electromagnetic media, metamaterials, and photonic systems. This includes neural-network-based emulation, inverse design, and accelerated simulation pipelines for complex electromagnetic environments.

Technical competencies include computational physics, data science, reproducible research pipelines, numerical simulation, mathematical modeling, and interdisciplinary research across physics, neuroscience, and complex systems. Applied experience spans R&D projects, national research grants, and collaborative academic environments.

Teaching and mentoring experience includes undergraduate instruction in optics and relativistic physics, curriculum design, technical communication, assessment development, and structured knowledge transfer. Additional work includes coaching high-performance students in analytical reasoning, problem decomposition, and performance optimization under time constraints.
